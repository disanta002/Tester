How is the Coverage?
- Koodo is a subsidiary of Telus.
- Telus and Bell have a partnership to offer coverage Canada-wide. There are no zones like Wind (Freedom) or Mobilicity.

What's included in the plans?
- See Plans

Can I keep my existing number?
- Yes you can!

Is there a contract with this plan?
- No, these are BYOD month-to-month post-paid plans, which don't have cancellation fees, and you can quit whenever you want.

Are these out-of-province plans?
- The plans that are offered here have pricing that is usually only available in certain provinces in Canada. This is due to regional carrier competition.

Is it illegal to have these plans?
- Not at all. These practices have been going on since 2013, and have made several news headlines.
​- They are currently grey-area, and Telus (Koodo) doesn't take action because it still generates revenue and subscribers for them.

Can I get a phone (on tab) with these plans?
- Tabs are not compatible with these plans. It's suggested to get a phone outright and unlocked to use with these plans
​
Can I meet you in person? 
- There is no need to meet in person, since the process is done mostly online.

How do these plans get set up? How do I pay after?
- The first step is to get a temporary Month-to-Month plan and phone number from Koodo/Telus
- Provide me with the details required to change your plan to your plan of choice
- Wait till the end of the partial billing cycle (2-9 days)
- I change your plan and bring your current number to Koodo/Telus (port)
- I will send you a link to pay me through PayPal.
​-These are the basic guidelines, Get the full set of instructions from Setup.

How much is your setup fee?
- I have the lowest setup fee in the market currently, which is $50 or $80 per line, depending on which plan you choose.
​- This can be paid through a PayPal link which I will provide you after the setup which I prefer the most. You can also pay the fee through an Interac bank eTransfer, or by Bitcoin (upon request).

Why is your setup fee $50/$80 whereas others charge $100+?
- Competitors are charging way too much for the same process that is done by Drew's Plans. There is no catch.

I live in Nova Scotia, Quebec, PEI, New Brunswick, Newfoundland. Why do I need to get an Ontario, Alberta, or British Columbia number?
- At the moment it is the only way the system works to change to these plans.

What is a port?
- A port is the process of bring a phone number from one cell carrier to another.
- Numbers can only be with one carrier at a time.
​
Do I need to cancel my current cell service subscription with my provider?
- No. Once the number is ported to Koodo/Telus, it automatically cancels your current plan with them and any remaining balance on the account will appear on your next final bill from them.
​
Is it okay to use my actual address?
- Yes. Do not falsify any information when you talk to anyone from Koodo/Telus.

​Can I get a new phone with these plans?
- It is suggested that you use your own unlocked phone. Koodo offers phones on 'Tab', but you will have to put a device on tab when you get the month-to-month plan. It is not certain that the tab is compatible with any of the plans that I offer.

I signed up for the $25 bill credit, but I don't see a discount on my bill
- After the plan is set up, don't forget to confirm your number with them with the email they sent you after signing up.
- The Koodo Refer-A-Friend process can take up to a month to apply towards your bill balance

​How long does it take to get set up?
- It can take anywhere from 2-9 days after getting the SIM card for the plan to be setup. It all depends on the billing cycle Koodo/Telus puts you on.
​
I am already with Koodo/Telus, how can I get any of these plans?
- Please follow the instructions at http://www.drewsplans.com/setup to get started! Your number can be kept.

Do I need to have my own unlocked phone or Can I get one from Koodo on a Tab?
- I would recommend you buy your phone out right but if you wish to, you may get a Tab to get a new phone at the time of getting your temporary plan.

My first bill is higher than you stated, what the heck?
- The monthly plan is the rate plan plus tax 
- All customers who sign up before the end of the month will incur partial monthly charges, called prorated charges. These will only appear on your first bill.
- If you were double-billed, this will be automatically corrected on the next month. I suggest you pay the double, then get the next month "free".
